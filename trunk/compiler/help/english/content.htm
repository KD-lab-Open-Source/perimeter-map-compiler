Additional Content

Perimeter Map Compiler supports some additional content for maps. Your can add a Hardness Map, a Zero Layer, a Surface Texture, a Sky texture, and 16-bit heightmap. To use the 16-bit heightmap support, simply convert the heightmap you use into a 16-bit grayscale format. The other content has to be turned on in the Project Settings dialog. Note that additional content increases the size of the final Shrub, so do not include any content you do not use. Now, about each kind of content in detail.

Hardness Map

This is a black and white image. It is black in those areas in which the terrain cannot be modified by the player, and white in others.

Zero Layer

This is another black and white image. This time, black defines those areas that are terraformed when the game starts. Players can build on such areas right away.

Surface Texture

This is a small texture that is applied to terraformed terrain in the game. The default texture gives terrain the typical scratched look.

Sky Texture

This is the texture of the sky in the game. Even though normally you cannot look up while playing, the sky is reflected in powered zero layer.

16-bit Heightmap

This is a feature new to Perimeter Map Compiler 1.2. Normal, 8-bit, heightmaps allow you to work with only 255 height levels. 16-bit heightmaps give you 8191 level to work with. They allow you to define the shape of the map that much more precisely.

Unfortunately, there are no good free image editors with adequate 16-bit image support yet. The later versions of Corel PhotoPaint and Adobe Photoshop can handle them.
